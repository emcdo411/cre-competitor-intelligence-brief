Why CBRE carries the heavier gap

Not a capability shortfall. CBRE has more deployed AI surface area than JLL: 39 billion data points, 1 billion square feet under SmartFM, its own LLM, Ellis AI running across hundreds of solutions.

The gap is sequencing. JLL spent years normalizing data country-by-country before layering AI on top. CBRE's own CEO, Bob Sulentic, said concrete evidence of the data-to-decision payoff won't exist until the end of 2026 — a future-tense claim about the exact same foundational problem JLL's CEO already called solved.

Under a Fractional CXO subtraction audit: CBRE is buying and building AI faster than it is finishing the architecture AI depends on. That's fixable. It is not a talent problem.

The Diagnosis

Four things are actually breaking CBRE's AI delivery right now. None of them are "CBRE doesn't have AI." All of them are named, sourced, and dated.

01 — Insight-Only, Not Decision-Driver

The payoff is dated to the future, by CBRE's own CEO.

Sulentic said real evidence of AI-driven gains — cheaper data, more efficient brokers — won't exist until the end of 2026. Under MOC's Phase 2 operationalization test, that's a self-admission of AITG > 50% today.

SOURCE: CBRE Q1 2026 earnings commentary — [PUBLIC-VERIFIED]
02 — Data Quality, Confirmed In-House

CBRE's own research names the constraint CBRE is trying to fix.

55% of respondents in CBRE's 2026 Global Workplace & Occupancy Insights cite data quality issues and lack of expertise as the biggest AI integration hurdles. Rare case of a vendor's own research confirming its own T2 constraint.

SOURCE: CBRE 2026 Global Workplace & Occupancy Insights — [PUBLIC-VERIFIED]
03 — Structural Delivery Bottleneck

The AI infrastructure buildout is capped by power, not by CBRE.

Interconnection timelines for AI-scale data campuses now run 24, 36, even 48+ months, driven by substation and transmission buildout. A T4 constraint entirely outside CBRE's execution, but one that caps how fast AI-driven demand converts to revenue.

SOURCE: CBRE 2026 U.S. Real Estate Market Outlook — [PUBLIC-VERIFIED]
04 — Market Repricing Ahead of Evidence

Investors moved on expectations, not on performance.

The February 11, 2026 "AI scare trade" hit CBRE, JLL, and Cushman & Wakefield together, despite strong quarterly earnings across all three. Analysts called it a repricing of future disruption risk — a criteria shift the market priced before any firm re-architected around it.

SOURCE: Market coverage, Feb 2026 selloff — [PUBLIC-VERIFIED]

The 30-Day Protocol

Every week run through two lenses on purpose. CXO without product discipline stays theoretical. Product thinking without CXO rigor ships something nobody asked for. Tap a week to open it.

W1 Diagnose +
Fractional CXO Lens — DBL + ORC

Classify every public CBRE AI claim (Ellis AI, Vantage Analytics, Next Action Engine, Capital AI) as AI Theater, Partial Adoption, or Embedded System using the daily-use / decision-driving / feedback-loop test. Desk research and internal conversation, no build yet.

D&T Product Analyst Lens

Same inputs, different question: which product has the clearest existing owner and instrumentation already in place? That's what's actually fixable inside 30 days.

OUTPUT → One-page capability inventory, tagged and ranked, with one circled candidate.
W2 Scope +
Fractional CXO Lens — Fraser Gate / Subtraction Audit

Before adding anything: what manual step is currently standing in for the missing feedback loop? Naming that exposes the real cost already being paid, often worth more than the new build.

D&T Product Analyst Lens

Draft the scoreboard spec: define "decision-driving" as a testable metric — e.g. % of broker recommendations acted on without manual override, or time from insight surfaced to action taken.

OUTPUT → One-page spec: single metric, data source, one named owner. No vague ownership.
W3 Prototype +
Fractional CXO Lens — ORC Read-Across

Test adoption friction, not just feasibility. Will people actually change behavior because of this scoreboard, or does it become a report nobody opens — AI Theater with better UX.

D&T Product Analyst Lens

Build the smallest version that produces a real number, even manually pulled. Honest answer using real CBRE data: insight-only, or decision-driving, right now.

OUTPUT → A working baseline measurement. A number, not a slide.
W4 Present +
Fractional CXO Lens — Board-Level Frame

Translate the finding into Sulentic's own words: is CBRE on pace for "concrete evidence by end of 2026," or not — based on this one product's real baseline.

D&T Product Analyst Lens

Present as a roadmap artifact: baseline, gap to target, two or three next moves, named owner for each. Show the trade-offs. Show what gets cut to make room.

OUTPUT → Single-page readout tied directly to the CEO's own public commitment.
