## The core question

> "Who makes better decisions faster because of AI, and where is the structural gap?"

That's different from "who has better AI." Capability inventory is step one. Whether that capability actually reaches the decision layer is the diagnostic.

---

## Diagnosis: four things breaking CBRE's AI delivery

### 01 — Insight-only, not decision-driver
CBRE CEO Bob Sulentic said concrete evidence of AI-driven gains (cheaper data acquisition, more efficient brokers) won't exist until the end of 2026. Under the MOC Phase 2 operationalization test, a future-tense payoff claim from the company's own CEO is a self-admission that today's output is largely insight-only, not decision-driving.
`[PUBLIC-VERIFIED]` — CBRE Q1 2026 earnings commentary.

### 02 — Data quality, confirmed in-house
CBRE's own *2026 Global Workplace & Occupancy Insights* found that 55% of respondents cite data quality issues and lack of expertise as the biggest AI integration hurdles. A vendor's own research confirming its own structural data constraint.
`[PUBLIC-VERIFIED]` — CBRE 2026 Global Workplace & Occupancy Insights.

### 03 — Structural delivery bottleneck (exogenous)
Interconnection timelines for AI-scale data campuses now run 24, 36, even 48+ months, driven by substation and transmission buildout, not by CBRE's execution. It caps how fast AI-driven infrastructure demand converts to revenue regardless of internal AI maturity.
`[PUBLIC-VERIFIED]` — CBRE 2026 U.S. Real Estate Market Outlook.

### 04 — Market repricing ahead of evidence
The February 11, 2026 "AI scare trade" hit CBRE, JLL, and Cushman & Wakefield together, despite strong quarterly earnings across all three. Analysts described it as a repricing of future disruption risk, a criteria shift priced by the market before any firm re-architected around it.
`[PUBLIC-VERIFIED]` — market coverage of the Feb 2026 selloff.

## AWSS Phase 5 — Amazon Benchmark Scorecard

Amazon scores 4/4 on every dimension by definition (the fixed Delta / North Star). Scale: 0 = not present, 1 = emerging, 2 = functional, 3 = scaled, 4 = Amazon-level (real-time, self-correcting, embedded in every decision layer).

| Dimension | Amazon | CBRE | JLL | Gap (CBRE) | Gap (JLL) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Data unification / single source of truth | 4 | 2 | 3 | 2 | 1 |
| Decision-driver vs insight-only | 4 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 2 |
| Feedback loop architecture | 4 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 2 |
| Operator Easy Button coverage | 4 | 2 | 3 | 2 | 1 |
| Governance / criteria validity (CGM) | 4 | 1 | 1 | 3 | 3 |
| **Total (of 20)** | **20** | **7** | **11** | **13** | **9** |

**Gap tier reference:** 0–8 Closing · 9–16 Structural lag (architecture investment required, not just tooling) · 17–24 Systemic · 25–32 Critical.

**Result:** CBRE = 13/20 (Structural lag). JLL = 9/20 (Structural lag, closer to closing).

### Why CBRE carries the heavier gap
Not a capability shortfall. CBRE has *more* deployed AI surface area than JLL: 39 billion data points, 1 billion sq ft under SmartFM, its own LLM, and Ellis AI running across hundreds of solutions `[PUBLIC-VERIFIED]`. The gap is sequencing. JLL spent years normalizing data country-by-country before layering AI on top; CBRE is layering AI on top of a data foundation its own CEO says will only be provably ready by end of 2026. Under a Fractional CXO subtraction audit, CBRE is buying and building AI faster than it is finishing the architecture AI depends on. That is a fixable sequencing problem, not a talent problem.

## AWSS Phase 6 — Competitive positioning

**Q1 — Who is winning today, and why?**
JLL, on the dimension that matters most to a product executive building "data platforms that drive decisions at scale": data unification. Completed-tense claim vs future-tense claim on the identical underlying problem.

**Q2 — Real advantage or perceived?**
Both real, and both partial. CBRE's advantage is deployment breadth `[PUBLIC-VERIFIED]`. JLL's advantage is architecture sequencing `[PUBLIC-VERIFIED]`. Neither is pure narrative; CBRE optimized for breadth first, JLL optimized for foundation first.

**Q3 — Where is the gap widening?**
Governance / Criteria Validity (CGM row): both firms score 1/4. Neither has public evidence of transaction-level drift detection. This is the widest true gap in the whole comparison, wider than the CBRE–JLL gap itself, and nobody on either side has a named owner for it.

**Q4 — Where does disruption risk exist?**
An AI-native CRE data platform that skips both firms' legacy-brokerage cost structure entirely. That is the threat the February 2026 selloff was actually pricing.

## AWSS Phase 8 — 12-month stress test

**Simulation:** CBRE forced to operate at Amazon-level AI standards within 12 months.

- **Q1 — Does CBRE survive the transition?** Partial. MLOps posture reads as *Emerging*, not *Absent*: real production platform (Ellis AI), real scale (1B sq ft), a named executive owner (Sandeep Davé, CDTO). But the feedback-loop layer needed for self-correction isn't confirmed live yet. Emerging can't hit Amazon-level in 12 months regardless of budget.
- **Q2 — Which constraint breaks first?** T2 (Data), not T1 (People). CBRE's own client research already names this constraint about itself, 55% citing data quality as the top blocker.
- **Q3 — What fails first?** Technology first (T2 dominant): breadth of data sources without confirmed unification; feedback loops unconfirmed as closed per CBRE's own leadership timeline.
- **Q4 — Intervention timing?** The constraint is on a public clock. Sulentic's own "end of 2026" marker means the window to name this gap, and have a 30-day first proof point ready, is now, before the claim is publicly confirmed or contradicted on a future earnings call.

## The 30-day dual-lens protocol

Every week run twice on purpose: the Fractional CXO lens asks *what's the binding constraint and who owns removing it*; the D&T Product Analyst lens asks *what ships, who uses it, does it move a metric*.

| Week | Fractional CXO Lens | D&T Product Analyst Lens | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — Diagnose** | Classify every public CBRE AI claim (Ellis AI, Vantage Analytics, Next Action Engine, Capital AI) as AI Theater / Partial Adoption / Embedded System (DBL + ORC) | Same inputs, ask which product has the clearest existing owner and instrumentation already in place | One-page capability inventory, tagged and ranked, one circled candidate |
| **2 — Scope** | Fraser Gate subtraction audit: what manual step is standing in for the missing feedback loop? | Draft the scoreboard spec: define "decision-driving" as a testable metric | One-page spec: single metric, data source, one named owner |
| **3 — Prototype** | ORC read-across: will behavior actually change, or does this become an unopened report? | Build the smallest version that produces a real number, even if manually pulled | A working baseline measurement, a number, not a slide |
| **4 — Present** | Board-level frame: is CBRE on pace for Sulentic's own "concrete evidence by end of 2026" claim? | Present as a roadmap artifact: baseline, gap to target, next owners, trade-offs shown | Single-page readout tied directly to the CEO's own public commitment |

**Why both lenses matter for the interview specifically:** a plan that's pure product process reads as a task list. A plan that's pure CXO diagnosis with no artifact reads as consulting, not the job. This protocol works because it's a CXO's question answered with a product analyst's discipline: ship something small, real, and tied to a number CBRE's own leadership already put on the record.

Every talking point above run through the actual three-layer evaluation used by the CBRE Product Executive driving this hiring decision.

Bob Sulentic put a date on that himself, end of 2026. I'd want to be the person who built the scoreboard for that claim in my first 30 days, not the person who found out the answer from the earnings call like everyone else."

| Test | Verdict | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — AI Relevance** | ✅ PASS | Names a specific, falsifiable technical distinction tied to a real deployed system, not a hypothetical. |
| **2 — CRE Value Connection** | ✅ PASS | Directly anchored to CBRE's own CEO's own public commitment; only makes sense inside CBRE's actual 2026 trajectory. |
| **3 — Product Executive Alignment** | ✅ PASS | Roadmap thinking with a trigger date and implied ownership; shows initiative toward measurement infrastructure. |
